Guidline :

Because there is a correlation between physical strength and size, competitors compete in weight classifications to ensure that matches and competitions are fair. Players are also required to dress appropriately, with them wearing short sleeve or sleeveless shirts and sports pants with little or no advertising. No jeans are allowed to be worn and neither is any kind of arm, elbow, or wrist support.

  • Elbow pads - These are where each competitor places their elbow down to wrestle. They are padded to avoid discomfort and prevent injury.
  • Touchpads - These are the raised pads on opposite sides of the table that each respective competitor is trying to push his opponent’s hand onto.
  • Hand pegs - These are used by competitors to grip onto whilst wrestling, thus giving themselves, some added leverage.

Scoring :

In individual matches, scoring is very simple in that it is a straightforward win/lose format over one round. However, at the World Arm-wrestling Championships, there is a team championship in which points are awarded for each individual competitor's performance in their category and are joined with those of their country’s team. Therefore, in each weight class, the following points are awarded:

  • 1st - 10 points
  • 2nd - 7 points
  • 3rd - 5 points
  • 4th - 4 points
  • 5th - 3 points
  • 6th - 2 points
  • 7th - 1 point
